Projects

Super Star Clusters in nearby galaxies

Starburst galaxies contain a large number of intermediate-age massive clusters, known as Super Star Clusters (SSCs). These clusters are thought to be the progenitors of present-day globular clusters. M82, the prototypical starburst galaxy, contains nearly 400 SSCs in its disk and 300 in its central region

I have been studying the structure and possible evolution of these SSCs, considering the initial mass functions (CIMF) and some semi-analytical simulations. To characterize the structure of those SSCs I developed the code nProFit, which is still under development and adapted for LSST upcoming data

Variable stars

Variable stars are instrumental in measuring distance scales. In particular, RR Lyrae stars (RRLs) are among the most luminous and common variables. RRLs have been also used to trace old and metal-poor populations. However, recent studies have challenged this traditional conception, suggesting that there is a fraction of young and metal-rich RRLs.
